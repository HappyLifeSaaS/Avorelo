The local viewer

Avorelo's viewer runs on your own machine and reads files that Avorelo wrote there. This page is a static explanation of what that viewer shows. It is not the viewer, and it is not connected to anything.

What this page is, and what it is not

  • The real viewer reads files generated on your machine, from your own work.
  • This website does not load your data. There is no account and no service to load it from.
  • No information is fetched from Avorelo. This page requests no data at all.
  • Everything below is an illustration. The numbers and rows are fixed examples written into the page.
  • You reach the actual viewer through the local command-line workflow, shown at the bottom of this page.

What the viewer actually shows

Each area maps to something Avorelo writes to disk while you work.

Local receipts

A record of what Avorelo did during a session: what it prepared, what it checked, and what it changed. Written as files in your project.

Local evidence

The artifacts behind a claim — command output, check results, validator output — kept alongside the receipt that refers to them.

Current local work

What the current session is doing, and the context carried into it, visible while you work and without sending the session anywhere.

Local history

Earlier sessions in the same project, so you can see what happened before and what carried forward.

Local status

Whether Avorelo is active in this project, what it detected, and what it will and will not touch.

Control Center

The local surface that ties these together, served from your own machine when you run the CLI.

How to open the real viewer

The viewer is part of the local command-line workflow. It is served from your machine, reads your local files, and needs no account.

avorelo status
Shows whether Avorelo is active in the current project, and what it detected.
avorelo open
Opens the local receipts viewer for the current project, from local files only.

Public installation instructions will be published when the Community Edition is released. Until then this page describes the workflow rather than pointing at a package or repository that is not available yet.
